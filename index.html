Statistical Measures: Location and Dispersion

Assignment prepared by: Guliyeva Mahbuba

1. Measures of Location (Central Tendency)

These measures identify the central point of a data set. The most common are the Mean, Median, and Mode.

Measure Definition Best Use (Advantage) Key Limitation
Mean ($\bar{x}$) The arithmetic average of all values. Symmetrical, non-skewed numerical data (Interval/Ratio). Uses all data. Highly sensitive to outliers.
Median The middle value in an ordered set (50th percentile). Skewed data or data with outliers. Robust measure. Does not use all observations.
Mode The most frequently occurring value. Categorical (Nominal) data to find the most popular category. Can be non-existent or multiple.

2. Measures of Dispersion (Variability)

These measures quantify the spread of data points from the central value, indicating consistency or variability.

Measure Definition Best Use (Advantage) Key Limitation
Range Max value - Min value. Quick, initial spread assessment. Only uses two values; highly sensitive to outliers.
Standard Deviation ($\sigma$ or $s$) Square root of the Variance. Units are the same as the data. Used with the Mean for symmetrical data (Normal Distribution). Sensitive to outliers.
Interquartile Range (IQR) $Q3 - Q1$ (Spread of the middle 50%). Skewed numerical data. Robust to extreme outliers. Ignores extreme 50% of data.

3. Advanced Topic: Practical Use of Recursive Calculation

Recursive (or "online") formulas are essential for real-time data processing and computational efficiency because they allow statistics to be updated incrementally as new data arrives, eliminating the need to store the entire dataset.

A. Recursive Mean Formula

$$\bar{x}_{n+1} = \bar{x}_n + \frac{x_{n+1} - \bar{x}_n}{n+1}$$

The term $(x_{n+1} - \bar{x}_n)$ is the error (residual). The new mean $\bar{x}_{n+1}$ is the old mean $\bar{x}_n$ plus a small adjustment proportional to this error.

Practical Applications of Recursive Mean:

  • Financial Markets: Instantly update the average stock price with every new trade without latency.
  • IoT Sensor Data: Continuously calculate the running average of metrics like temperature or pressure from millions of sensors without overwhelming memory.

B. Recursive Sum of Squares ($S_n$) for Variance/Standard Deviation

$$S_{n+1} = S_n + (x_{n+1} - \bar{x}_{n+1})(x_{n+1} - \bar{x}_n)$$

This formula updates the Sum of Squared Differences ($S_n$), which is the core component of variance. $S_{n+1}$ represents the sum of squares after $n+1$ observations, correctly building upon $S_n$, the sum after $n$ observations.

Practical Applications of Recursive Variance:

  • Quality Control: Real-time monitoring of variability (Standard Deviation) in manufacturing processes. An immediate increase in variance signals that the process is out of control.
  • Anomaly Detection: Tracking the spread of network latency or system load. A sudden change in standard deviation (even if the mean is stable) can indicate an abnormal event or a cyber threat.
  • Numerical Stability: This method is preferred in computational systems as it is more numerically stable and less prone to floating-point rounding errors than the traditional two-pass method when dealing with very large datasets.
