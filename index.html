Statistical Measures: Location and Dispersion

Assignment prepared by: Guliyeva Mahbuba

1. Measures of Location (Central Tendency)

These measures identify the central point of a data set. The most common are the Mean, Median, and Mode.

Measure Definition Best Use (Advantage) Key Limitation
Mean ($\bar{x}$) The arithmetic average of all values. Symmetrical, non-skewed numerical data (Interval/Ratio). Uses all data. Highly sensitive to outliers.
Median The middle value in an ordered set (50th percentile). Skewed data or data with outliers. Robust measure. Does not use all observations.
Mode The most frequently occurring value. Categorical (Nominal) data to find the most popular category. Can be non-existent or multiple.

2. Measures of Dispersion (Variability)

These measures quantify the spread of data points from the central value, indicating consistency or variability.

Measure Definition Best Use (Advantage) Key Limitation
Range Max value - Min value. Quick, initial spread assessment. Only uses two values; highly sensitive to outliers.
Standard Deviation ($\sigma$ or $s$) Square root of the Variance. Units are the same as the data. Used with the Mean for symmetrical data (Normal Distribution). Sensitive to outliers.
Interquartile Range (IQR) $Q3 - Q1$ (Spread of the middle 50%). Skewed numerical data. Robust to extreme outliers. Ignores extreme 50% of data.

3. Advanced Topic: Recursive Calculation

In data streams, calculations must be done *online* without storing the entire dataset. This utilizes **recurrence formulas**.

Recurrence Formula for Mean

$$\bar{x}_{n+1} = \bar{x}_n + \frac{x_{n+1} - \bar{x}_n}{n+1}$$

This method drastically reduces the memory and computation required for continuous updates.
